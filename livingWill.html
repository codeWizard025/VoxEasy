Living Will

Purpose

A Living Will outlines a person’s preferences regarding medical treatments and life-sustaining measures in the event they become incapacitated and unable to communicate their wishes.

Declaration of Living Will

I, [Your Full Name], being of sound mind, make this statement as a directive to be followed if I become unable to participate in decisions about my medical care. These directions express my legal right to refuse or accept medical treatment.

1. Designation of Health Care Proxy (Optional)

If I become unable to make decisions about my medical care, I designate the following person as my health care proxy:

  • Name: _____________________________
  • Relationship: _____________________________
  • Contact Information: _____________________________

2. Statement of Desires, Special Provisions, and Limitations

  • Life-Sustaining Treatments: I direct that life-sustaining treatments, including but not limited to mechanical ventilation, artificial nutrition and hydration, and CPR, be withheld or withdrawn if I am in an irreversible vegetative state or if the treatments would only prolong the dying process.
  • Pain Relief: I wish to receive adequate pain relief, even if it may hasten the moment of my death, provided that the intent is solely to relieve pain and not to cause death.
  • Organ Donation (Optional): Upon my death, I wish to donate any organs or tissues that may help others in need. [Specify any particular organs or tissues if desired.]
  • Additional Instructions: [Any other specific wishes regarding medical care.]

3. Revocation of Previous Directives

I hereby revoke any prior Living Wills or advance directives.
